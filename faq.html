Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about sexuality, relationships, and personal safety

Q: Is it normal to feel confused about my sexuality?

Yes, it's completely normal to feel confused or uncertain about your sexuality, especially during adolescence. Everyone's journey of self-discovery is different, and it's okay to take your time figuring things out. Remember, sexuality can be fluid and may change over time.

Q: How do I know if I'm ready for sex?

Being ready for sex is a personal decision that depends on many factors. Some signs of readiness include:

  • Feeling comfortable with your body
  • Being able to communicate openly with your partner
  • Understanding the risks and responsibilities involved
  • Feeling emotionally prepared

Remember, there's no rush - it's okay to wait until you feel completely ready.

Q: What should I do if I'm being pressured to send nude photos?

It's important to remember that you should never feel pressured to do something you're not comfortable with. If someone is pressuring you to send nude photos:

  1. Say no firmly and clearly
  2. Explain your boundaries
  3. If the pressure continues, consider blocking the person
  4. Seek help from a trusted adult or report the behavior to the appropriate authorities

Remember, once an image is sent, you lose control over who sees it or where it ends up.

Q: What should I do if I think I've been sexually assaulted?

If you think you've been sexually assaulted, it's important to seek help immediately:

  1. Go to a safe place
  2. Tell someone you trust
  3. Consider seeking medical attention
  4. Contact a sexual assault helpline for support and guidance
  5. Consider reporting the incident to the police

Remember, it's not your fault, and there are people who can help you.

Q: How do I know if I'm in a healthy relationship?

A healthy relationship is based on mutual respect, trust, and open communication. Some signs of a healthy relationship include:

  • Feeling safe and comfortable with your partner
  • Being able to express your feelings and opinions freely
  • Having your boundaries respected
  • Supporting each other's individual growth and interests
  • Resolving conflicts through open and honest communication

Q: How can I talk to my parents or guardians about sex and relationships?

Talking to parents or guardians about sex and relationships can feel awkward, but it's important. Here are some tips:

  • Choose a quiet time when you won't be interrupted
  • Be honest about your questions or concerns
  • Start with less sensitive topics and build up to more personal ones
  • Use resources like books or websites as conversation starters
  • Remember that they were once your age too

If you're not comfortable talking to your parents, consider speaking with a trusted adult, school counselor, or healthcare provider.

Q: What should I do if I'm being bullied because of my gender or sexuality?

If you're being bullied, it's important to remember that it's not your fault and you don't have to face it alone. Here are some steps you can take:

  1. Tell a trusted adult, such as a parent, teacher, or school counselor
  2. Document the incidents if possible
  3. Don't retaliate - this can make the situation worse
  4. Know your rights - many schools have anti-bullying policies in place
  5. Seek support from friends or LGBTQ+ youth organizations
  6. Consider contacting a helpline for additional support
