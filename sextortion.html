Understanding Sextortion

Learn about the risks, impacts, and how to protect yourself from sextortion.

What is Sextortion?

Sextortion is a form of blackmail where someone threatens to share intimate images or information about you unless you meet their demands. This can happen to anyone, regardless of age, gender, or background.

Key Facts

  • Sextortion often starts on social media or dating apps
  • Perpetrators may use fake profiles to gain trust
  • Demands can include money, more explicit content, or sexual favors
  • It's a crime and can have serious legal consequences for perpetrators

Impact on Victims

Sextortion can have severe consequences for victims:

  • Emotional distress and anxiety
  • Feelings of shame and isolation
  • Damage to relationships and reputation
  • Academic or work performance issues
  • In extreme cases, it can lead to self-harm or suicidal thoughts

What Would You Do?

Your friend tells you they've been chatting with someone online who's now threatening to share their private photos. What's your advice?

How to Protect Yourself

  1. Be cautious about sharing personal information or intimate content online
  2. Use privacy settings on social media and dating apps
  3. Be wary of people you've only met online, especially if they pressure you for personal information or images
  4. Trust your instincts - if something feels off, it probably is
  5. Remember that you're not alone and it's not your fault if you become a victim

If You're Being Sextorted:

  1. Don't panic - take a deep breath and remember it's not your fault
  2. Don't comply with the demands - this often leads to more threats
  3. Stop all communication with the perpetrator
  4. Save all evidence (messages, emails, etc.) but don't keep explicit images
  5. Tell a trusted adult or contact a support service
  6. Report the incident to the police and the eSafety Commissioner

If you're dealing with sextortion or know someone who is, help is available.
